Timeline

This website will be focused on the various types of instruments that are used from each of the 7 continents. Throughout the years, the concept of music has changed as the world itself has changed. There are over 1,500 musical instruments that are played around the world. These range from the simple Neanderthal flute (60,000 years old) to the newest Hango (4-5 months old). This page will give information and resource links to each continent and information on some of the instruments they use. I hope you find this information to be as interesting as I did.

Comparison

While every continent has its own unique instruments, some instruments share similar aspects from region to region. Take the american guitar, for example. In South America, they have what they call a Charango, which resembles the North American acoustic guitar. Some places like Antarctica have little options for musical instruments. As a result they find ways to create their own way of producing music. For more information on instruments and their origin, check out the links to the left.
